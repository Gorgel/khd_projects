1. The Minimum Solar Mass Nebula (MSMN)¶

Q:

The mass of the disk that formed the solar system planets is unknown. However, it is possible to estimate a lower limit to the mass the disk must have had by using the observed masses of the planets and scale them up to roughly solar abundance (i.e. “add” an appropriate mass of hydrogen & helium). Estimate the radial distribution of the MSMN by using the following procedure:

(a) Starting from the inferred masses of heavy elements (such as iron) in the planets, add enough mass of H and He to bring the composition up to solar abundance values.

(b) Spread the masses in annuli centered on the current semi-major axes of the planets and extending half-way to the neighbouring planets, and compute the surface density $\Sigma$ (mass per area, in the unit kg m$^{−2}$ ).

(c) Plot the resulting $\Sigma$ as a function of distance in a log-log diagram, and find the best fit for a function $\Sigma(r) = kr^{−\alpha}$ (where $k$ and $\alpha$ are fitted constants and $r$ is the radius) by fitting a straight line to the plotted values.

2. Dust-gas dynamics¶

Q:

The orbital velocity of gas in an axisymmetric circum- stellar disk is not only dependent on the gravity from the central star, but also on the gas pressure gradient. The midplane radial equation of motion for gas in cylindrical coordinates is thus

$$ \ddot{r}-r \dot{\varphi}^2 = -\frac{GM_{\star}}{r^2} - \frac{1}{\rho}\frac{\partial P}{\partial r} $$

where $r$ and $\varphi$ are the cylindrical radius and azimuthal angle respectively, $G = 6.67 \cdot 10^{−11}$ m$^3$ kg$^{−1}$ s$^{−2}$ is the gravitational constant, $M$ is the stellar mass, $P$ is the gas pressure, and $ρ$ is the gas mass density. Assume that the gas is isothermal in circular orbit around the star, and that the radial midplane density is $\rho(r) = \rho_0(r/r_0 )^{−\alpha}$ .

(a) What is the orbital speed $v_{gas}(r) = r\dot{\varphi}(r)$ of the gas as a function of radius $r$? Use the isothermal equation of state.

(b) A dust grain of size s is orbiting at Keplerian speed $v_{dust}(r)$ at radius $r$, and so will experience a relative velocity with respect to the gas, giving rise to “Epstein drag"

$$ F_{drag} = -\frac{4}{3}\pi s^2 \rho v_{rel} v_{th} $$

where $v_{rel} = v_{dust} - v_{gas}$ is the relative velocity between the dust and the gas, and

$$ v_{th} = \sqrt{ \frac{8 k_B T}{\pi \mu m_H} } $$

is the thermal speed of the gas molecules, where $\mu$ is the mean molecular weight, $k_B = 1.38 \cdot 10^{−23}$ J K$^{−1}$ is the Boltzmann constant, and $m_H = 1.66 \cdot 10^{−27}$ kg is the hydrogen atom mass. Let the stopping time be defined by

$$ t_{stop} = \frac{m v_{rel}}{F_{drag}} $$

where $m = 4\pi s^3 \rho_{dust} /3$ is the mass for a spherical dust grain of size s and density \rho{dust}. What is the stopping time as a function of the radius $r$ and dust grain size $s$? What is the dimensionless stopping time $T{stop} = \dot{\varphi} t_{stop} ?

(c) Compute the dimensionless stopping time $T_{stop}$ for the specific case $s = 2 \mu m, r = 1$ AU, $T = 100$ K, $M_{\star} = 1 M_{\odot}$ , $r_0 = 1$ AU, $\alpha = 7/2$, $\rho_0 = 2 \cdot 10^{−12}$ kg cm$^{−3}$ , $\rho_{dust} = 3 \cdot 10^3$ kg cm$^{−3}$, and $\mu = 2$.

3. Gravitational instability¶

A criterion commonly used for determining disk stability against gravity is the Toomre criterion that states that a disk annulus can start to fragment when

$$ Q \equiv \frac{c_S \Omega}{\pi G \Sigma} < 1 $$

where $c_S$ is the sound speed, $\Omega$ is the orbital frequency, $G$ is the gravitational constant, and $\Sigma$ is the surface mass density.

(a) Using reasonable assumptions, compute $Q$ as a function of radius for the MSMN.

(b) Compute the critical surface density as a function of radius (i.e. for $Q = 1$) and compare it to the MSMN.

(c) A ‘clump’ in a disk will resist shear if it is Roche unstable, that is, if

$$ \rho > \rho_{Roche} \sim \frac{3.5 M_{\star}}{r^3} $$

where $\rho$ is the volume mass density, $r$ is the radius and $M_{\star}$ is the mass of the central star. Using some reasonable assumptions (e.g. for the scale height), compute the Roche critical surface density $\rho$ Roche and compare it to the MSMN and Toomre critical densities above.

a)

I assume isothermal equation of state with sound speed as

$$ c_S = \sqrt{\frac{R T}{\mu}}. $$

I also assume that the angular frequency can be expressed in terms of the gas speed in problem 2

$$ \Omega = \frac{v}{r} = \frac{1}{r} \sqrt{ \frac{G M_{\star} }{r} -\frac{R T \alpha}{\mu} } $$

and I assume that the surface density can be described as the one derived in problem 1

$$ \Sigma(r) = 52.352\cdot r^{-1.751}. $$

Finally I assume that $T = 100$ K, $\alpha = 7/2$, $\mu = 2$ and $M_{\star} = M_{\odot}$. With this I calculate $Q$ as a function of $r$ below:
